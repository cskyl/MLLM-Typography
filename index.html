Overview

The page should tell the scientific story in under ten seconds.

This concept treats the project page as a research narrative, not just a repository landing page. The first screen makes the core vulnerability visually obvious, the next section compresses the main claims into high-confidence takeaways, and later sections expand into methods, ablations, figures, and safety implications.

The page is framed around one memorable idea: speech is a native, realistic attack surface for audio-visual MLLMs, and it can redirect both audio-grounded and visually grounded reasoning.
Cross-modal framing Present audio, visual, and text attacks as three coordinated streams instead of three isolated benchmarks.
Figure-first storytelling Use the paper figures as centerpiece artifacts so the page feels anchored in the actual science.
Public-release ready Present authors, affiliation, and preprint access directly in the hero while keeping the visual composition clean and uncluttered.
Premium visual system Dark editorial surfaces, soft glows, modality accents, and restrained motion create a fancy but credible aesthetic.

Method

Turn the paper into a clean visual pipeline.

Method sections on project pages often become dense. Here the solution is a five-step visual pipeline: clean input, speech generation, attack construction, model evaluation, and safety analysis.

1

Start from clean audio-video inputs

Use a calm baseline card with neutral lighting and clean spacing to represent the original multimodal scene before perturbation.

2

Inject spoken target cues

Represent TTS-based perturbation with waveform motifs and parameter chips for volume, repetition, temporal placement, and voice identity.

3

Compose cross-modal conflicts

Show audio, visual, and text streams as coordinated rails so visitors can immediately understand aligned and conflicting attack settings.

4

Evaluate ACC and ASR

Convert the benchmark story into readable cards rather than raw tables. Emphasize targeted redirection, not just overall degradation.

5

Stress safety-sensitive moderation

Close the method pipeline with a warmer, more urgent card to preview why this matters beyond benchmark performance.
